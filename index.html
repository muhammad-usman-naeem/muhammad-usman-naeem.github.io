I am a PhD candidate in Economics and Public Policy at Tufts University. My research is in development, environmental, and behavioural economics. I am also a Fellow in the Department of Economics at Harvard University.

My work uses field experiments to study the psychological and economic forces shaping electricity markets, air pollution, and climate policy in low-income countries, with a focus on Pakistan.

Previously, I was a Country Economist at the International Growth Centre.
