I am a PhD candidate in Economics and Public Policy at Tufts University. I am also a Fellow in the Department of Economics at Harvard University.

My research is in development, environmental, and behavioural economics. My work uses field experiments to study the psychological and economic forces shaping electricity markets, air pollution, and climate policy in low-income countries, with a focus on Pakistan.

I work closely with government and private-sector partners to design and test policy solutions in the field. Previously, I was a Country Economist at the International Growth Centre.
