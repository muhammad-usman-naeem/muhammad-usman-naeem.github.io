Job Market Paper

The Behavioural Economics of Vehicular Idling

This paper studies excessive vehicle idling in Lahore, Pakistan, and evaluates whether low-cost behavioural interventions can reduce unnecessary fuel use and emissions. Using field experiments and high-frequency GPS data, it examines the roles of information, incentives, and inattention in shaping idling behaviour.

Status: Writing | AEA Registry

Working Papers

Experimental Price Variation under Nonlinear Electricity Tariffs: Evidence from Pakistan

(with Robin Burgess, Tim Dobermann, Michael Greenstone, and Faraz Hayat)

This paper studies how households respond to anti-theft messaging and pricing incentives in electricity markets. It uses administrative billing data and field experimental variation to examine losses, payment behaviour, and the scope for improving utility performance.

Status: Writing | AEA Registry

Preaching Against Power Theft: Experimental Evidence from Pakistan

(with Robin Burgess, Tim Dobermann, Michael Greenstone, and Faraz Hayat)

This paper studies the effect of mosque-based anti-electricity-theft messaging in Pakistan, using experimental variation in exposure to sermons and linked administrative electricity outcomes.

Status: Writing | AEA Registry

(with Robin Burgess, Tim Dobermann, Michael Greenstone, and Faraz Hayat)

This project studies whether technology deployment and audit interventions can reduce theft and improve utility performance in Pakistan's power sector.
