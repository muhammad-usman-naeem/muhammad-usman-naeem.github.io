Job Market Paper

The Behavioural Economics of Vehicular Idling

This paper studies excessive vehicle idling in urban Pakistan and asks whether low-cost behavioural interventions can reduce unnecessary fuel use and emissions. Using field experiments and high-frequency GPS data, it examines the effects of information, incentives, and in-vehicle reminders on drivers’ idling behaviour.

Status: Fieldwork | AEA Registry

Working Papers

Experimental Price Variation under Nonlinear Electricity Tariffs: Evidence from Pakistan

(with Robin Burgess, Tim Dobermann, Michael Greenstone, and Faraz Hayat)

This paper studies how households in rural Pakistan respond to nonlinear electricity prices when theft and off-grid alternatives such as solar are available. Using experimental price variation and administrative utility data, it estimates demand and theft responses to price and examines how households substitute across legal grid use, theft, and solar.

Status: Writing | AEA Registry

Preaching Against Power Theft: Experimental Evidence from Pakistan

(with Robin Burgess, Tim Dobermann, Michael Greenstone, and Faraz Hayat)

This paper studies whether shifts in social norms can reduce electricity theft in areas where formal enforcement is weak. Using experimental variation in mosque-based anti-theft messaging in Pakistan, it examines whether religious and community-based persuasion can improve payment behaviour and support utility performance.

Status: Writing | AEA Registry

Technology, Enforcement, and Power Theft in Pakistan

(with Robin Burgess, Tim Dobermann, Michael Greenstone, and Faraz Hayat)

This project studies whether technology or enforcement is more effective at reducing electricity theft in Pakistan’s power sector. In partnership with LESCO and the Ministry of Energy, it compares theft-resistant infrastructure and enhanced audits using high-frequency administrative data on losses, payment, and consumer regularisation.

Status: Fieldwork | AEA Registry

Industrial Emissions Ratings and Disclosure

(with Michael Greenstone)

This project studies whether credible third-party emissions information and public disclosure can improve environmental performance among industrial plants in Lahore. In collaboration with the Punjab Industries Department and the Punjab Urban Unit, it generates plant-level emissions ratings for selected industries and tests whether public disclosure encourages cleaner practices and pollution-control investment.

Status: Design

The Environmental Costs of Small-Scale Farming

(with Steve Cicala and Kyle Emerick)

This project studies whether coordination failures and scale diseconomies lead small farmers in Punjab, Pakistan, to burn crop residue rather than collect it. Using a pre-pilot centred on coordinating neighbouring plots for mechanised residue clearing, it examines whether reducing coordination and collection costs can make cleaner residue management economically viable.

Status: Design
